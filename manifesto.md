title: Manifesto
---

I approach work as a process of careful design.

My background in software development taught me to value structure, consistency, and clear boundaries. Well-designed systems should be easy to understand, predictable in behavior, and resilient to change.

I prefer architectures that are simple at their core and flexible at the edges. Complexity should be intentional, localized, and justified by real needs—not added for its own sake.

I pay close attention to how systems evolve over time. Readability, maintainability, and thoughtful trade-offs matter more to me than novelty or scale alone.

Across software, filmmaking, and photography, my approach remains consistent: observe closely, reduce noise, and make deliberate choices. Good design, in any medium, is rarely loud—but it is precise.
